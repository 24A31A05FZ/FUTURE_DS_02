# INSIGHTS & RECOMMENDATIONS REPORT
## Customer Retention & Churn Analysis
### Telco Dataset | Analytics Team | June 2026

---

## SECTION 1: 15 BUSINESS INSIGHTS

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 1: Contract Length Is the Strongest Churn Predictor
**Finding:** Month-to-month contract customers have a **42.7% churn rate**, compared to 11.3% for one-year and only 2.8% for two-year contracts.
**Implication:** Commitment level is the single most reliable indicator of long-term loyalty. The difference between month-to-month and two-year churn is **15×**.
**Business Action:** Create compelling upgrade offers (discounts, bonuses) to move customers to annual plans.

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 2: The First 12 Months Are Make-or-Break
**Finding:** Over 50% of churned customers leave within their first 12 months. Churn rate for 0–12 month tenure group is **47.7%**.
**Implication:** The onboarding phase is the highest-risk period. Once a customer passes the 2-year mark, churn drops dramatically to under 10%.
**Business Action:** Deploy dedicated onboarding specialists and automated engagement workflows for new customers.

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 3: Electronic Check Payment = Churn Signal
**Finding:** Customers paying via electronic check churn at **45.3%**, nearly double the average rate of 26.5%.
**Implication:** Electronic check users may be less committed to the service, or they may face payment friction that increases dissatisfaction.
**Business Action:** Implement an auto-pay incentive program (e.g., $5/month discount for bank transfer or credit card enrollment).

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 4: Fiber Optic Internet Users Churn More than DSL Users
**Finding:** Despite being the premium service tier, Fiber Optic customers churn at **41.9%**, while DSL users churn at only **19%**.
**Implication:** Premium customers have higher expectations. Fiber Optic may have service quality issues, billing surprises, or competitive pressure.
**Business Action:** Conduct root-cause interviews with churned Fiber Optic customers. Invest in service reliability SLAs and dedicated support queues.

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 5: Online Security Subscription Cuts Churn in Half
**Finding:** Customers without Online Security churn at **41.8%**; those with it churn at only **14.6%** — a **35-point difference**.
**Implication:** Add-on services create stickiness. Security products create emotional investment (fear of losing protection).
**Business Action:** Bundle Online Security into base plans, or offer a free 90-day trial to all new subscribers.

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 6: Tech Support Is a Retention Anchor
**Finding:** Customers without Tech Support churn at **41.6%** vs. **15.2%** for those with it.
**Implication:** Customers who have access to help feel supported and are significantly less likely to leave.
**Business Action:** Proactively promote TechSupport enrollment for high-risk customers. Include it in onboarding packages.

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 7: Paperless Billing Has a Surprising Churn Link
**Finding:** Paperless billing customers churn at **33.6%** vs. **16.3%** for those with paper billing.
**Implication:** Paperless customers may be more tech-savvy and willing to switch, or they notice billing details more easily.
**Business Action:** Segment paperless users and monitor their billing inquiry frequency as a leading churn indicator.

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 8: Senior Citizens Churn Less Than Expected
**Finding:** Senior citizens (SeniorCitizen=1) churn at **25.5%** — slightly below the overall average for certain contract types.
**Implication:** Seniors on long-term contracts are among the most loyal customer groups.
**Business Action:** Develop senior-targeted retention programs with simplified billing and dedicated support lines.

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 9: Customers with Dependents Are More Loyal
**Finding:** Customers with dependents churn at **15.5%**, versus **31.3%** for those without dependents.
**Implication:** Family obligation creates stability. These customers need multi-device, family-plan features.
**Business Action:** Develop "Family Plans" with shared data, bundled services, and multi-user discounts to increase stickiness.

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 10: Streaming Services Drive Engagement But Not Retention Alone
**Finding:** StreamingTV and StreamingMovies subscribers churn at **30%+** — above the average.
**Implication:** Streaming subscriptions alone don't create loyalty; they are often paired with month-to-month contracts.
**Business Action:** Bundle streaming with annual contract offers. Create exclusive streaming + security package deals.

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 11: High Monthly Charges Amplify Churn Risk
**Finding:** Customers in the Premium charge tier (>$95/month) churn at nearly **35%**.
**Implication:** Value perception decreases at higher price points, especially when service reliability is inconsistent.
**Business Action:** Implement value-justification campaigns for high-spend customers — show them ROI through usage dashboards.

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 12: Revenue Loss Is Concentrated in Month-to-Month Segment
**Finding:** Month-to-month churned customers account for **78% of all monthly revenue lost** despite being ~55% of the base.
**Implication:** Targeting just the month-to-month high-spenders for retention would yield outsized revenue impact.
**Business Action:** Create a "VIP Retention Team" focused exclusively on high-value month-to-month customers.

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 13: Customers with Multiple Services Show Higher CLV
**Finding:** Customers subscribing to 5+ services have 2.3× higher CLV than single-service customers.
**Implication:** Product bundling is a powerful CLV accelerator and churn reducer.
**Business Action:** Create structured bundle packages (e.g., Starter, Professional, Enterprise) to maximize service adoption.

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 14: Two-Year Contract Customers Are Your Growth Engine
**Finding:** Two-year contract customers have the highest average CLV (~$4,500+) and lowest churn rate (2.8%).
**Implication:** These are your most profitable, most stable customers. They are also most likely to refer others.
**Business Action:** Create a referral loyalty program that rewards long-term customers for bringing in new business.

---

### 📌 INSIGHT 15: Early Identification of High-Risk Customers Is Feasible
**Finding:** 6 risk factors (contract type, tenure, monthly charges, tech support, security, payment method) can accurately flag **High Risk** customers with ~85% precision.
**Implication:** Rule-based segmentation can serve as a cost-effective pre-ML churn alerting system.
**Business Action:** Deploy a real-time Risk Score dashboard in CRM to trigger automatic outreach for score ≥ 6.

## SECTION 2: 10 ACTIONABLE RETENTION STRATEGIES

---

### 🎯 STRATEGY 1: Contract Conversion Campaign
**Type:** Commercial Initiative  
**Target:** All Month-to-Month customers with tenure > 3 months  
**Mechanism:** Offer 15–20% annual discount, 2 months free, or device upgrade incentive  
**Expected Impact:** Reduce M2M churn by 20–30%; add $40K+/month in secured revenue  
**Timeline:** Launch within 60 days; run quarterly refresh campaigns

---

### 🎯 STRATEGY 2: Customer Onboarding Success Program
**Type:** Customer Success Initiative  
**Target:** All new customers (0–90 days)  
**Mechanism:** Assign a Customer Success Manager; week-1, week-4, week-12 check-ins; automated email nurture sequence with feature tips  
**Expected Impact:** Reduce early churn (0–12 months) from 47.7% to under 30%  
**Timeline:** Build playbook and tooling in Q3; pilot with 500 new customers

---

### 🎯 STRATEGY 3: Auto-Pay Incentive Program
**Type:** Billing & Payments Initiative  
**Target:** All electronic check users  
**Mechanism:** $5/month discount for switching to bank transfer or credit card auto-pay  
**Expected Impact:** Reduce electronic-check churn from 45% to under 30%  
**Timeline:** Implement in billing system within 30 days

---

### 🎯 STRATEGY 4: Proactive Risk Outreach System
**Type:** Predictive Analytics Initiative  
**Target:** All customers with RiskScore ≥ 6  
**Mechanism:** Automated CRM alert triggers call from retention specialist + personal offer within 72 hours  
**Expected Impact:** Save 15–25% of high-risk customers; protect ~$30K/month in revenue  
**Timeline:** Integrate risk scores into CRM within 45 days

---

### 🎯 STRATEGY 5: Security & Support Bundling
**Type:** Product Strategy  
**Target:** All customers missing OnlineSecurity or TechSupport  
**Mechanism:** Offer 60-day free trial of both services; auto-convert to paid unless opted out  
**Expected Impact:** Reduce churn for un-secured customers from 41.8% to under 20%  
**Timeline:** Product bundling rollout in Q3

---

### 🎯 STRATEGY 6: Family & Multi-Device Plans
**Type:** Product Strategy  
**Target:** Customers with dependents or multiple phone lines  
**Mechanism:** Launch "Family Plan" with shared GB allowance, family discounts, parental controls  
**Expected Impact:** Increase dependents-segment retention from 84.5% to 92%+  
**Timeline:** Product design in Q3; launch Q4

---

### 🎯 STRATEGY 7: Loyalty Rewards Program
**Type:** Loyalty & Engagement Initiative  
**Target:** Customers with tenure > 24 months  
**Mechanism:** Points system rewarding tenure milestones, referrals, and service adoption; redeemable for bill credits or upgrades  
**Expected Impact:** Reduce loyal-customer churn by 50%; increase NPS and referral rate  
**Timeline:** Partner with loyalty platform vendor; launch within 90 days

---

### 🎯 STRATEGY 8: Fiber Optic Service Quality Initiative
**Type:** Operations & Product Quality  
**Target:** All Fiber Optic subscribers  
**Mechanism:** Dedicated Fiber support line, proactive outage notifications, SLA guarantees, monthly satisfaction surveys  
**Expected Impact:** Reduce Fiber churn from 41.9% to under 25%  
**Timeline:** SLA framework in 45 days; dedicated support team in 60 days

---

### 🎯 STRATEGY 9: Personalized Re-Engagement for At-Risk Customers
**Type:** Marketing & CRM Initiative  
**Target:** Customers who haven't logged in or used service in 30+ days  
**Mechanism:** Personalized email/SMS with feature spotlight, exclusive "come back" offer, usage report showing value delivered  
**Expected Impact:** Re-engage 20–25% of dormant accounts before they churn  
**Timeline:** CRM segmentation and email automation setup within 30 days

---

### 🎯 STRATEGY 10: Annual Business Review (ABR) for High-Value Customers
**Type:** Customer Success / Account Management  
**Target:** Top 20% customers by MonthlyCharges (>$80/month)  
**Mechanism:** Quarterly business reviews presenting personalized usage analytics, upcoming features, and exclusive previews  
**Expected Impact:** Reduce premium customer churn from 35% to under 15%  
**Timeline:** Define ABR playbook in 30 days; begin with top 200 accounts
